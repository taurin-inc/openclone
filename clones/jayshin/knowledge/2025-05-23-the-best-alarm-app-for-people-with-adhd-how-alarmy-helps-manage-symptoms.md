## ADHD: A Hidden Struggle in Modern Life

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a **neurodevelopmental disorder** characterized by inattention, impulsivity, and hyperactivity. While once considered an issue primarily affecting children, recent studies show that **adult ADHD** is also widespread and can **impact all areas of daily life**.

Approximately 2.5–4% of adults worldwide are affected by ADHD. Including those who struggle without a diagnosis, the actual number is likely much higher. In today’s fast-paced world, with its overwhelming multitasking and complex schedules, **people with ADHD face enormous challenges** in maintaining daily rhythm and structure.

## Real-World Challenges Faced by People with ADHD

### 1) Getting up in the morning is extremely difficult
People with ADHD often **struggle to wake up with ordinary alarms**. Their brain’s arousal system functions differently, making it hard to transition out of deep sleep. Even when they hear the alarm, they may reflexively turn it off and fall back asleep, resulting in **frequent tardiness, disorganized schedules, and heightened stress**. This is not merely a matter of willpower but a **neurological trait**.

### 2) Poor time perception and frequent missed appointments
People with ADHD **struggle to intuitively track the passage of time**, making it hard to judge how long something takes or when to start preparing. This often leads to being late for important meetings or appointments. They may lose all sense of time when focused on one task, which can **damage work reliability and personal relationships**.

### 3) Chronic procrastination
Procrastination in ADHD is **not about laziness**. Weak executive function impairs their ability to prioritize and break tasks into manageable steps. Faced with important but tedious or complex tasks, they often feel overwhelmed and delay starting. This leads to last-minute scrambles, **excessive stress, and lower quality outcomes**.

### 4) Frequently forgetting important tasks
**Poor working memory** is a core ADHD symptom. People often forget things they just remembered a few minutes ago. Even if they make notes, they may forget to check them. During multitasking, this worsens, resulting in missed appointments or repeated mistakes that **undermine self-efficacy and trust**.

### 5) Difficulty maintaining routines
Due to sensitivity to new stimuli, ADHD users often **find consistent routines boring and struggle to stick to them**. Even if they try to build healthy habits, they soon lose track, impulsively change plans, or get distracted, making it **hard to pursue long-term goals**.
