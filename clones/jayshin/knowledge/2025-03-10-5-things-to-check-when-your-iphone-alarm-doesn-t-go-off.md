# 5 Things to Check When Your iPhone Alarm Doesn’t Go Off 🔔📱

**Date:** 2025-03-10  
**Author:** (재)행복나눔재단 SIT(Social Innovators Table)팀

Have you ever set an alarm, only to wake up late because it never rang? ⏰😱

There are several reasons why your iPhone alarm may not go off as expected, including **sound settings, Focus mode, Bluetooth connections, and software issues**.

To help you troubleshoot the issue quickly, we’ve put together **a checklist of 5 things to check when your iPhone alarm doesn’t go off**. Follow these steps to fix the problem and never miss an alarm again! 🚀

---

## **✅ 1. Check Sound & Haptics: Ringer and Alarm Volume**

On an iPhone, **the media volume (for music and videos) and the alarm volume are separate settings**.

If the ringer and alarm volume are too low or if silent mode is activated, your alarm might not be loud enough to wake you up.

### **📌 How to check**

1. **Go to Settings → Sounds & Haptics → Ringtone & Alarm volume**
2. Increase the volume to the **maximum level**
3. **Disable "Change with Buttons"** to prevent accidental volume changes

📌 Even if your iPhone is in silent mode, the alarm should still go off. However, if the volume is too low, you might not hear it!

---

## **✅ 2. Make Sure Your Alarm Sound Is Not Set to ‘None’**

One of the most common reasons why alarms don’t make any sound is that the **alarm sound is set to ‘None’**.

If this happens, the alarm will go off at the scheduled time but will remain silent.

### **📌 How to check**

1. Open the **Clock app → Alarms → Edit (✏️) → Select an alarm**
2. Check if the **"Sound"** option is set to `"None"`
3. Choose a different ringtone and save the changes

📌 If your alarm is set to ‘None’, it will go off but without any sound. Make sure to pick an actual ringtone!

---

## **✅ 3. Check Focus Mode (Do Not Disturb & Sleep Mode)**

Your iPhone has a **Focus Mode (Do Not Disturb, Sleep Mode, etc.)**, which can **mute alarms under certain conditions**.

### **📌 How to check**

1. **Go to Settings → Focus → Do Not Disturb / Sleep Mode**
2. Check if `"Allow Alarms When Focus is On"` is disabled, and turn it on if necessary
3. Make sure Focus Mode is **not automatically scheduled**

📌 Focus Mode **should not affect alarms by default**, but certain settings might interfere with them, so it's best to double-check!

---

## **✅ 4. Check Bluetooth Connection**

If your iPhone is connected to **Bluetooth headphones, AirPods, or an external speaker**, your alarm **might ring through the connected device instead of your iPhone speaker**.

If you're not wearing your Bluetooth earbuds, you might not hear the alarm at all.

### **📌 How to check**

1. **Go to Settings → Bluetooth → Disconnect all devices**
2. **Turn off Bluetooth completely and test your alarm**
3. Check if your iPhone is stuck in headphone mode

📌 If you’re using wired Lightning earphones, unplug and reconnect them to ensure the alarm sounds properly.

---

## **✅ 5. Check for Software Issues (iOS Updates)**

Some iOS versions have had **alarm-related bugs** where alarms wouldn’t go off or remained silent.

Updating to the latest iOS version can often fix these issues.

### **📌 How to check**

1. **Go to Settings → General → Software Update**
2. If a new iOS update is available, **install it and test your alarm**
3. If the issue persists, **restart your iPhone**
