# 4 Effective Habits for Insomnia

**Date:** 2025-03-12  
**Author:** Alarmy / SIT (Social Innovators Table) Team

Everyone experiences sleeplessness occasionally. Even those blessed with good sleep genes sometimes toss and turn all night. You might find it difficult to fall asleep at times, but it's usually not a major concern. Perhaps you became engrossed in something and missed your bedtime, consumed too much caffeine that day, or simply had one of those nights when sleep is elusive for no particular reason. In most cases, if you endure this period, you'll soon return to your normal sleep patterns.

However, when insomnia becomes chronic, it should be taken as a warning sign. If you find it difficult to fall asleep or maintain quality sleep several times a week for months, this is not a simple problem. Chronic insomnia can severely reduce your quality of life. It can be a sign of depression and make sleep feel like a burdensome task rather than a form of rest. The more difficult it is to fall asleep, the worse your mental health becomes.

## **What Causes Insomnia?**

Like most problems that become easier to solve once you identify their causes, treating insomnia works the same way. Stress, irregular sleep patterns, poor sleep habits, mental health issues such as anxiety and depression, physical ailments and pain, medication effects, neurological abnormalities, and various sleep disorders are all common causes of insomnia. Many people experience insomnia due to a combination of these factors.

**Common symptoms of insomnia include:**

*   Difficulty falling asleep at night
*   Waking up frequently during sleep
*   Waking up too early
*   Not feeling rested after sleep
*   Daytime drowsiness or fatigue
*   Anger, sadness, or irritability
*   Decreased concentration, attention, or memory
*   Increased mistakes or accidents
*   Persistent worries about sleep
*   Melatonin deficiency

## **4 Effective Habits for Insomnia**

### **1. Avoid Napping**

On days when you haven't had enough sleep and struggle to stay awake at your desk, a short nap might seem like a quick way to restore energy. Generally, brief naps of about 15-30 minutes are known to boost energy and reduce stress.

Unfortunately, if you have sleep apnea or insomnia, even that short nap might have the opposite effect. For people with certain sleep disorders, napping can worsen symptoms and trap you in a cycle of sleeplessness. The more sleep you get during the day, the less tired you feel at night, which can exacerbate insomnia symptoms. Avoiding naps helps build up "sleep debt," which aids in getting quality sleep at night.

### **2. Get Plenty of Sunlight During the Day**

Our bodies are designed to function according to the sun's rhythm. When the sun sets, our bodies produce melatonin, the sleep hormone, and when it rises, production of this hormone decreases.

Many modern people spend all day indoors. However, research shows that getting sufficient sunlight significantly helps improve physical and mental resilience, increases productivity, and enhances mental health. This has effects in preventing depression and improving sleep quality.

### **3. Consume Foods Rich in Melatonin**

Melatonin is known as the sleep hormone and plays a key role in regulating our body's circadian rhythm. Without sufficient melatonin, it takes longer to fall asleep and it's difficult to get quality sleep. Even without supplements, there are foods that naturally increase melatonin levels:

*   **Cherries** - A fruit known for its high melatonin content
*   **Bananas** - Rich in melatonin, magnesium, and tryptophan
*   **Walnuts** - Rich in melatonin and antioxidants
*   **Milk** - Contains tryptophan, which helps with quality sleep
*   **Oats, goji berries, mushrooms** - Natural sources of melatonin

### **4. Listen to Sleep-Inducing Music Before Bed**

Quiet and calm music can help settle your mind and make it easier to fall asleep. Gentle sleep music has the effect of calming the autonomic nervous system, slowing breathing, and lowering heart rate and blood pressure.

Music can counteract negative associations with the bedroom, stop anxious thoughts, and bring about the physical and mental calm needed for sleep. Natural sounds like wind or water, white noise, or various types of meditation music or ASMR might be suitable depending on the individual.

## **Conclusion**

Practicing good sleep habits not only helps treat insomnia but also makes your daily life easier and more efficient. Neglecting sleep management increases the risk of various health problems and can shorten your lifespan. If you're having trouble falling asleep at a consistent time, definitely consider the four lifestyle habits mentioned above.
