<Korean startup scene 2.0>

Not really sure whether it's 2.0 or 3.0 but there is a drastic shift of focus coming in the Korean startup scene.

One undeniable fact is that the Korean market is shrinking in the long run. Whether we like it or not, our population is set to decrease with a whopping 0.78 birthrate. What exacerbates this even further is that our population is aging like no other country, well, probably Japan is a step ahead of us but we will catch up very soon.

Not surprisingly, young talents are trying to start a career outside of Korea in places such as the States, Canada and Singapore. I truly wish them their very best but it will take some time to settle down and adjust to the new norm.

So what am I trying to say?

ALL Korean unicorns (except games) are Korea focused. #Kakao #Naver #Toss #WoowaBros etc.

This is going to change. This is set to change.

Korean startups with business models and talent that are more 'international' will have a higher chance to survive and succeed. Sure they can start out as a Korea focused business, but I think they need to have a clear path to going global. This is a trend observed quite easily these days but will be even more important going forward.

So do we have a good chance?
