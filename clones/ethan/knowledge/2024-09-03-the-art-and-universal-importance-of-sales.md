<The art of sales> 

One thing that I hadn't done a lot throughout my career was sales.

First of all, I started out my career in accounting & finance, so wasn't exactly positioned for a sales job. Yes, I did participate from time to time in making powerpoint decks for pitches, but that was about it.

I think the first time I seriously tried to sell something hard was during my business school days at #columbia . Job interviewing season was a huge thing, and we had to get very good at selling ourselves.

Asian norms, especially Korean and Japan emphasize modesty & humbleness over everything, which doesn't help very much for a salesperson in the beginning, but we had to overcome that to market ourselves and our skillsets on #Wallstreet and elsewhere.

Fastforward to today and I'm doing a lot more sales than I expected. I think at the end of the day, everything is sales. Either you're on the job market selling yourself, or you will have to sell your image or company's image to fight for something hard to get. If you're not fighting for it, chances are that it's not something valuable.

I firmly believe that sales is an art. You really have to understand what your selling as well as why the potential buyer would ever care about buying. It's a psychology game, and the default is failure, which makes it even more fun (and stressful tbh).

All great startups founders are great salespersons.
However, the greatest startups somehow have all their members as sales persons.
Something to strive for, something to achieve down the road.
