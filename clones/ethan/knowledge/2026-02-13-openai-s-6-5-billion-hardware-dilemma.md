# OpenAI's $6.5 Billion Hardware Dilemma

### When AI Software Isn't Deep Enough to Justify a Device

**Author:** 애당초 미디움-레어 (Ethan Cho)  
**Date:** Feb 13, 2026

OpenAI’s first AI hardware device has been pushed to 2027. Peter Welinder, VP and General Manager, confirmed the delay in court filings related to a trademark dispute with audio startup iyO (Wired, Feb 10, 2026). Nearly two years after acquiring Jony Ive’s consumer hardware subsidiary io for $6.5 billion — the largest M&A in OpenAI’s history — the company hasn’t even produced packaging materials.

This isn’t a scheduling hiccup. It exposes a structural contradiction at the heart of OpenAI’s strategy.

**The MAU Trap: Wide but Not Deep**

ChatGPT boasts approximately 810 million monthly active users (Sensor Tower, Nov 2025). Impressive on the surface. But the underlying metrics tell a different story.

ChatGPT’s app market share collapsed from 69.1% to 45.3% in just twelve months (Apptopia, Jan 2026). Gemini surged from 14.7% to 25.2%. Grok leapt from 1.6% to 15.2%. MAU growth has effectively stalled — only 6% over four months from August to November 2025 (Sensor Tower).

The more revealing metric is time spent. According to Apptopia’s January 2026 data, the highest average time spent per daily active user belongs not to ChatGPT but to Claude, at 34.7 minutes. ChatGPT and Copilot are both trending downward.

User profiles confirm the divergence. Similarweb data shows Claude users overlap heavily with GitHub, Stack Overflow, and Notion — the tools of builders and power users. ChatGPT users overlap with YouTube, Instagram, and gaming platforms — the profile of casual, mainstream consumers (GPTrends, 2025).

What I call the “MAU Trap” is this: massive user counts that mask shallow engagement. The AI market is already fragmenting by use case — coding migrates to Claude, workflow automation to Gemini, research to Perplexity. ChatGPT is converging toward a role as the entry point for first-time AI users. As Apptopia’s Tom Grant put it: “ChatGPT built the category, but as viable alternatives have scaled, users are naturally diversifying their toolkit.”

**The Strategic Contradiction**

OpenAI is now moving to monetize this wide, shallow user base through advertising and commerce. This is not inherently a bad strategy. Building an ad-supported platform on top of 800 million users is exactly the playbook that made Google and Meta what they are. It’s rational.

But here’s the question worth sitting with: what sits at the end of that strategic direction?

Advertising and commerce are fundamentally traffic businesses. You attract users broadly, keep them lightly engaged, and monetize the surface area. Hardware operates on the opposite logic entirely. A consumer purchases a dedicated device only when the dependency is deep — when they cannot imagine their day without it. The iPhone achieved this. AirPods achieved this.

A strategy that optimizes for broad, shallow traffic and a strategy that requires deep, irreplaceable dependency point in fundamentally different directions. OpenAI is currently building an ad platform with one hand and preparing a $6.5 billion hardware bet with the other. Whether those two hands are reaching for the same future is the question the company needs to answer.

**The Lesson Already Written**

Humane’s AI Pin and Rabbit R1 already delivered the verdict. Their failure wasn’t about manufacturing capability. It was that AI software hadn’t earned the right to exist as a standalone device. The software wasn’t essential enough to justify a separate piece of hardware in anyone’s pocket.

OpenAI faces the same wall. It has acquired $6.5 billion worth of design DNA from the man who shaped the iPhone. But unless the software experience inside that hardware becomes something users genuinely cannot live without, Jony Ive’s beautiful device will join the graveyard of elegant solutions to problems nobody had.

OpenAI’s real challenge isn’t the launch timeline. It’s making AI irreplaceable in daily life — solving the software depth problem first. The hardware can wait. In fact, it already is.
