Other than YC, is there a single example of a VC firm that’s actually built a community around their brand?

So many VCs brag about their “community building” with founders. But usually it’s just some generic events (dinners, conferences, retreats), bland content (blog posts on GTM and hiring, boring podcasts no one listens to), and Slack channels that die after a few posts.

YC’s the only firm I’ve seen that built a real community that’s self-reinforcing, wide, and still feels exclusive. Founders and alums stay tight, go out of their way to help and invest in each other, and years later proudly flex their batch year on all their socials. There’s this intense sense of belonging, like school spirit, that keeps spreading and lifts the YC brand worldwide.

VCs who throw around that term, listen up: what YC did is real community building.
