Why small, agile funds may outperform giants during downturns in VC fundraising cycles.

Big funds talk about ‘dry powder’ but end up slow and bureaucratic when shit hits the fan. Small funds? They know every dollar, every founder, every mistake—and can pivot in real time.

Most LPs chase brand names for comfort, not returns. Ironically, oversized rounds and herd instincts create the very instability they fear.

Want real alpha? Watch the underdogs who hunt in overlooked markets and write checks before consensus forms.

In downturns, it’s not about size. It’s about conviction, timing, and the stomach to ignore the crowd.

Are you still betting on legacy logos, or ready to back the next contrarian winner?
